Novak Djokovic

22 May 1987

Novak Djokovic (22 May 1987) born in Serbia is a Tennis Legend who started playing his beloved sport from the tender age of Four. He had been dominating the mens' tennis from 2011, winning against his greatest rivals Roger Federer and Rafel Nadal in multiple occasions. He is currently leading the G.O.A.T race after winning 23rd grand slam championship in Ronald Garros in June, 2023.

Early Career


Novak Djokovic showed great talent and passion for tennis from a young age. Djokovic began playing tennis at the age of four and quickly progressed in the sport.

In 2003, at the age of 16, Djokovic turned professional. He started participating in lower-level tournaments and quickly gained attention for his skills on the court. In the same year, he won his first ATP Challenger title in Budapest, Hungary.

Djokovic's breakthrough year came in 2006 when he reached the quarterfinals of the French Open, becoming the youngest player to reach that stage since Andre Agassi in 1998. Later that year, he reached the semifinals of Wimbledon, solidifying his position as one of the rising stars in men's tennis.

In 2007, Djokovic achieved his first Grand Slam success by reaching the final of the US Open. Although he lost to Roger Federer in the championship match, it marked a significant milestone in his career.
